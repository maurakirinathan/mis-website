MASTER OF CYBERSECURITY: DISTANCE MASTER PROGRAM IN CYBER SECURITY

INTRODUCTION

The Master of Information Security (MIS) is ideally suited for young graduate students who want to assume leadership positions in the information security arena.

This two year master program enhances a theory and technical education with 14 courses in information security and management. This courses covers the topics essential for the effective development of secure information systems.

Graduates of the MIS program will be qualified to occupy nearly all of the job roles in the area of information Security such as Chief Information Security Officer, Information Security Architect, Information Security Analyst, Incident Responder, Network Security Engineer, Forensic Analyst, Penetration Tester and Malware Analyst.

PROGRAM OBJECTIVES
  • Acquire a detailed understanding of information security challenges in networks and software systems
  • Assess the information security risks faced by an organization
  • Design and implement networked, software and distributed systems with information security in mind
  • Gain expertise in both theory and practice of information security
  • Gain expertise to manage the growing complexities associated with securing data and networks
  • Gain a detailed understanding of the interdisciplinary aspects (technical, business, management, policy) of information security

University of Colombo School of Computing (UCSC) is government own leading academic institute in the south Asian region in the area of computer science. In striving for excellence in education and research, the UCSC has always attempted to foresee the needs of the region and the developing world as a whole.

Competency of the academic staff is one of the crucial factors in determining the quality of the educational and research process. The UCSC presently has over 20 senior lecturers with  doctoral qualifications and a further 15 with masters qualifications.

Featured Lecturer

Dr. Kasun De Zoyza
Dr. Kasun De Zoyza was obtained his BSc in Computer Science from University of Colombo, Sri Lanka in 1998. He obtained his Ph.D. in the area of Computer Security from Stockholm University in 2003. After a brief period of post-doctoral work at the George Washington University, USA, he joined the University of Colombo School of Computing (UCSC), Sri Lanka in 2003. At present he works as a senior lecturer at UCSC, advisor for forensic investigation unit attached to UCSC and academic coordinator of Master of Science in Information Security Program at UCSC.
